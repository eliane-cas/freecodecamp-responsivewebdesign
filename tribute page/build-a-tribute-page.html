Primary modern proponent for the significance of symbiosis in evolution

Best known for:

  • Endosymbiosis theory: Her formative paper, "On the Origin of Mitosing Cells", appeared in 1967 after being rejected by about fifteen journals. Her theory that cell organelles such as mitochondria and chloroplasts were once independent bacteria was largely ignored for another decade, becoming widely accepted only after it was powerfully substantiated through genetic evidence
  • Symbiosis as evolutionary force: Strong critic of neo-Darwinism
  • Gaia hypothesis: Co-developer of the Gaia hypothesis with the British chemist James Lovelock Gaia Hypothesis: earth is a single self-regulating system
  • Five kingdoms of life: became one of its most important supporters and when in mid-2000s, she became its most important defender
